# Gender Inequality and Maternal Mortality

Interactive dashboard exploring the relationship between gender inequality
(GII / GDI) and maternal mortality across countries, focused on
identifying countries that deviate from what their inequality level would
predict — for better or worse.

**[Live demo HERE :)](https://health-equity-dashboard-nvtgt6fxzvgs58zm5cneua.streamlit.app/)**

📝 **[Read the full methodology walkthrough →](ANALYSIS.md)** — the reasoning behind every design decision in this project, written as a technical deep dive.

## Motivation

How much does gender inequality explain differences in maternal mortality
between countries? This project uses open data from [Our World in
Data](https://ourworldindata.org/) to explore that question, combining
interactive exploration, a log-linear regression model to quantify the
gap between expected and observed outcomes, and unsupervised clustering
to identify country profiles.

## Methodology

### Filtering aggregates
OWID CSVs include non-country entities (`World`, `Arab States (UNDP)`,
etc.). These are filtered using maternal mortality's region column
(`World region according to OWID`), which comes back **empty** for
aggregates by OWID's own design — filtering by `region.notna()` excludes
them without a hardcoded exclusion list or an additional country-to-
region mapping dataset.

### GII vs. GDI
They are not interchangeable:
- **GII** (0=parity, 1=high inequality): measures structural inequality
  in reproductive health, empowerment, and the labor market.
- **GDI** (~1=parity, >1 = women exceed men in HDI): is a human
  development ratio between genders, not a direct structural inequality
  measure. Its relationship with maternal mortality is empirical, not
  assumed to match GII's.

### Gap score
`log(maternal_mortality) ~ inequality_indicator` is fit over the selected
year's countries. Each country's *gap score* is the residual: how much
more or less maternal mortality it has than its inequality level would
predict, relative to that year's sample. It is neither an absolute nor a
causal measure.

### Clustering (K-means)
Countries are grouped by `[inequality_indicator, log(maternal_mortality)]`,
standardized. The number of clusters (k) is chosen by maximizing the
*silhouette score* between k=2 and k=6. Cluster labels are descriptive,
generated by comparing each centroid against the global median — with
k>2, two distinct clusters can share a label; in that case check each
one's composition, don't assume they're the same group.

## Limitations

- **Correlation does not imply causation.** The analysis is observational.
- The *gap score* and clustering use only two variables; they do not
  control for GDP per capita, healthcare access, or armed conflict.
- GII/GDI coverage is uneven across countries and years — more limited
  than maternal mortality's. Rows without an inequality indicator remain
  NaN and are excluded from the analysis for that year/country.
- The *gap score* is relative to the sample available in a given year, not
  directly comparable across years without adjusting for that.

## Deploying to Streamlit Cloud

1. Push this repo to GitHub.
2. Go to [share.streamlit.io](https://share.streamlit.io) and sign in with
   GitHub.
3. Click "New app", select the repo, branch (`main`), and main file
   (`app.py`).
4. Streamlit Cloud installs `requirements.txt` and deploys automatically.
